# PF-1 First Validation Program

## Goal

Determine quickly whether `PF-1` can become a real material program or should stay a strong internal concept only.

## Scope

This should be a **short bounded academic validation program**, not a scale-up effort.

## Recommended Structure

### Phase 1: Candidate Downselection

Pick 3 candidate families:

- 1 porous functional polymer sorbent family
- 1 hybrid bead / granule family
- 1 structured monolith / coated-substrate family

For each family, define only a small bounded variant set.

### Phase 2: First Bench Screen

Evaluate the first-pass candidates on:

- PFAS capture behavior
- pressure-drop behavior
- fouling tendency
- regeneration recovery after one short loop
- obvious mechanical or packing failure

### Phase 3: Repeat The Winner

Take only the strongest first-pass candidate and repeat it under the same bounded conditions.

The main question here is:

Can the early signal repeat without collapsing on fouling, regeneration, or hydraulic behavior?

## Minimum Useful Outputs

The professor should be able to produce, for each serious candidate:

- material family description
- why it was chosen
- bounded preparation route summary
- first measured capture outcome
- first measured regeneration outcome
- first measured pressure/fouling outcome
- decision: continue, retune, or kill

## Suggested Decision Gates

### Continue

- balanced early signal across capture, regeneration, and hydraulics
- no obvious collapse under one bounded replay

### Retune

- one strong dimension
- one weak but plausibly fixable dimension

### Kill

- repeated collapse in regeneration
- unacceptable hydraulic behavior
- obvious fouling fragility
- unworkable complexity relative to observed benefit

## What This Program Should Not Try To Do

- prove market superiority
- prove certification readiness
- prove full lifecycle economics
- produce a field-ready product

## Best-Case Outcome

At the end of this validation program, a professor should be able to say one of three things:

1. `PF-1` has a real material family worth deeper development.
2. `PF-1` is directionally interesting but needs reframing.
3. `PF-1` is not strong enough to justify further work.
