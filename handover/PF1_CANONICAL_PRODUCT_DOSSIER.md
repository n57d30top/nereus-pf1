# PF-1 Canonical Product Dossier

## One-Line Definition

`PF-1` is a **bounded closed-loop PFAS polishing module** for controlled industrial wastewater loops.

## What PF-1 Actually Is

`PF-1` is the primary surviving `Nereus` product candidate after the internal PFAS-first product-candidate, confirmation, review, decision, launch-review, and bounded benchmark chain.

This means:

- it is the current `Nereus` winner
- it is framed as one product candidate, not a whole platform claim
- it is meant for one conservative insertion point inside closed-loop industrial water handling

## Product Form

The cleanest current reading is:

- one PFAS polishing module or skid
- installed behind pre-treatment
- before final guard stages or recirculation return
- used only inside a controlled industrial wastewater loop

## First Buyer Picture

The first sensible buyer is not a consumer and not a municipal drinking-water operator.

The current buyer picture is:

- industrial PFAS remediation operators
- industrial process-water teams
- closed-loop wastewater operators needing one conservative polishing stage

## Why PF-1 Won Internally

`PF-1` won because it held the best **combined** posture across:

- capture efficiency
- fouling resistance
- regeneration discipline
- pressure behavior
- manufacturability posture

It did **not** win because of one exaggerated single metric.

## Evidence Chain

The current canonical support chain is:

1. product candidate
2. confirmation and second confirmation
3. product review refresh
4. commercialization tighten and prep
5. product decision
6. launch review
7. market-comparison matrix
8. bounded internal baseline benchmark execution

The practical meaning is simple:

- `PF-1` is the strongest internally confirmed `Nereus` candidate
- `HM-1` did not hold
- `MP-1` and `MP-2` did not overtake it

## What The Benchmarks Actually Say

The bounded internal baseline benchmark execution showed positive comparative posture against:

- `GAC`
- `ion exchange`
- `RO`
- one certification-relevant surrogate reference

This is enough to justify deeper material work.

It is **not** enough to claim:

- certified superiority
- public-water readiness
- real-world field advantage
- a quantified market percentage advantage

## Allowed Current Story

The current honest story is:

`PF-1` is a bounded internal PFAS-first product candidate with unusually strong internal comparative posture and enough coherence to justify real material-development work.

## Not Allowed Current Story

Do **not** describe `PF-1` as:

- a finished water-treatment product
- a certified PFAS filter
- a public-water-ready system
- a field-proven module
- a product with proven lifecycle economics
- a material with a finalized manufacturing recipe

## What Is Still Missing

The most important open gaps are:

- exact material instantiation
- bounded synthesis route selection
- bench-scale validation
- regeneration and fouling behavior in real matrices
- breakthrough and service-window characterization

## What A Professor Should Do Next

The right next step is not full productization.

It is:

1. choose the most plausible first PF-1 material family
2. instantiate a small bounded candidate set
3. run the first short validation program
4. decide quickly whether the concept survives contact with real materials
