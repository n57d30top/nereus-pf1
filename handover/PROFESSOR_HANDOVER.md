`PF-1` is a bounded closed-loop PFAS polishing concept that has become the strongest internal `Nereus` lead after repeated internal confirmation and bounded surrogate baseline benchmarking.

## Why You Are Receiving This

This repository is intended as a handover for a professor or senior materials collaborator who can assess whether `PF-1` deserves translation from a governed internal concept into a real material-development program.

## What Is Established

- `PF-1` is the primary surviving `Nereus` candidate.
- `HM-1` was retired.
- `MP-2` was retired as a secondary exploration path.
- `PF-1` has a closed bounded internal proof chain.
- `PF-1` has a bounded internal benchmark posture against `GAC`, `ion exchange`, `RO`, and a certification-relevant surrogate.

## What Is Not Yet Established

- no real-world performance claim
- no certification or compliance claim
- no field or pilot proof
- no finalized production recipe
- no demonstrated lifecycle economics

## What We Need From a Professor

- assess whether the underlying capture/regeneration tradeoff is scientifically plausible enough to justify real material work
- decide what material classes or synthesis families should be explored first
- define the minimum experimental program needed to validate or kill the concept quickly
- identify the most dangerous assumptions in the current bounded surrogate model

## Recommended Working Mode

Treat `PF-1` as a **technical hypothesis with unusually good internal structure**, not as a finished water-treatment material.
