# Technical Brief

## Core Framing

`PF-1` is framed as a **bounded closed-loop PFAS polishing module** for controlled industrial wastewater loops.

## Intended Placement

- behind pre-treatment
- before final guard stages or recirculation return
- inside a controlled closed-loop wastewater system

## Why PF-1 Survived Internally

Internally, `PF-1` became the main `Nereus` lead because it held the best combined posture across:

- capture efficiency
- fouling resistance
- regeneration discipline
- pressure behavior
- manufacturability posture

The important point is that it won on **balance**, not on one single spike metric.

## Benchmark Posture

Bounded internal execution currently shows positive posture against four market-analog baseline families:

- `GAC`
- `ion exchange`
- `RO`
- certification-relevant surrogate reference

This does **not** prove market superiority. It only means the concept is strong enough to justify deeper technical scrutiny.

## Most Useful Scientific Reading

The best way to read `PF-1` is:

- not as "the filter is already invented"
- but as "there is now a bounded architecture and score profile worth trying to instantiate in real material form"

## What Should Probably Be Explored in Real Work

- candidate material families for selective PFAS capture
- regeneration compatibility versus irreversible degradation
- pressure-drop behavior under practical packing and flow assumptions
- fouling sensitivity under realistic industrial matrices
- service-window and breakthrough behavior
